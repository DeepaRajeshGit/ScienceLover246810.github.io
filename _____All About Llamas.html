All About Llamas

Description

Llamas have long necks and thin limbs. They also have rounded muzzles and their upper lips are split. Their lower teeth stick out. Llamas can be in the colors white, brown, gray, black, or reddish-brown with yellow or white patches.

Habitat

The native habitat of llamas is the Andes Mountains. Now they are found in grasslands and farmlands. Unfortunately, llamas no longer live in the wild.

Diet

Llamas eat mountain vegetation, such as low shrubs and lichens.

Behavior

Llamas are very gregarious. There is one male and many females in a herd. All llamas are aggressive towards their predators. They bite, charge, kick, and spit to defend themselves.

Fun Facts

Llamas spit at each other and spit at people, too.
Llamas have unusual blood which helps them live at places with very low levels of oxygen.
Llamas are smart.
Llamas sometimes hum to communicate.
